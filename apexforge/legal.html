Legal & Compliance
Know before you mod

This page provides general safety and compliance guidance for car & bike modifications. It is not legal advice. Laws vary by country/state/city and can change over time.

Emissions & ECU changes

ECU remaps, catalyst changes, and exhaust modifications can violate emissions regulations and trigger inspection failures. Some areas allow “track-only” parts; others do not.

Rule of thumb: if a change affects emissions equipment (cats, O2 sensors, EVAP), assume it requires verification for your region and inspection program.
Noise

Exhaust volume limits are increasingly enforced. On bikes, full systems and short cans can quickly exceed legal thresholds.

Best practice: use resonators/quality mufflers, keep cats where required, and avoid “cold start” maps on street builds.
Lighting & visibility

DRL colors, underglow, tinted lamps, and headlight swaps may be restricted. Improper aim can be unsafe and illegal.

Avoid: prohibited colors on public roads, overly tinted lenses, and misaligned projectors.
Fitment & safety

Aggressive offsets, stretched tyres, and poorly secured aero can fail at speed. Suspension changes require alignment. Brake upgrades require correct bedding.

Critical: torque fasteners to spec, re-check after heat cycles, and never compromise wheel integrity.
